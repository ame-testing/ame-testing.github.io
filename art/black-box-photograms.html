Black box photograms

A row of black boxes - each one slightly larger than a human face - contains the photograms, which are framed together with metal rods and acrylic shapes that mimic the assemblages in the images. Shifting positions and angles within the confines of each box, these recurring elements produce uncertainty in one's sense of depth and distance. A prolonged gaze into each of the dark boxes renders the components increasingly ambiguous, thus allowing new formations to become perceptible.
